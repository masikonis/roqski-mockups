When should you hire a business litigation lawyer?

You should hire a business litigation lawyer when a dispute starts to put your money, control, or reputation at risk. Triggering events include receiving demand letters, lawsuits, or arbitration notices; being locked from business accounts by partners; witnessing misused funds or customer solicitation; or facing missed payments affecting operations.

Early intervention protects leverage, controls costs, and provides clear options before escalation. Court orders may be necessary to prevent ongoing harm, key records may require preservation, and contract clauses regarding arbitration, venue, or attorney fees could impact strategy.

Practical, business-driven strategy

Roquemore Skierski PLLC begins each matter by clarifying business objectives, litigation risk, timing constraints, and budget limits, then aligns pleadings, discovery, and motion practice to those priorities.

Litigation isn't the answer to every business problem. We seek early resolutions that serve client objectives without prolonged litigation. At the same time, opposing parties understand these attorneys prepare cases for trial when facts and law require it.

Damages and remedies available in commercial disputes

The starting point for many matters is compensatory damages — money making the injured party whole, including unpaid invoices, amounts due under agreements, or documented losses from breach of contract or fiduciary duty. Some cases support lost profits claims, requiring careful proof and calculation methods.

Equitable relief applies when disputes call for court-ordered action beyond financial compensation. Courts may issue injunctions restraining harmful acts and, in limited situations, order specific performance requiring contractual obligation fulfillment. When property or money was improperly taken, remedies may include restitution returning unjustly obtained assets.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I bring to my initial consultation?

Prepare a clear dispute timeline, the amount at issue, and your ideal resolution. Bring any relevant contracts, correspondence, demand letters, and financial documents related to the dispute.

What is the statute of limitations for common business claims?

In Texas, most breach of contract claims must be filed within four years. Fraud and certain debt collection claims also have four-year limits. Property injury claims usually have two-year limits. Some business-related claims may have shorter or longer deadlines, making quick action important for preserving your rights.

How much will business litigation cost?

Costs vary widely depending on case complexity, evidence quantity, witness numbers, and dispute duration. Simple cases may cost thousands; complex or prolonged litigation reaches tens or hundreds of thousands. Costs include attorney fees, court filing fees, expert witnesses, and evidence gathering and review expenses.

Can I recover attorney fees if I win?

Winning parties may receive attorneys fees compensation. Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code § 38.001 allows prevailing parties in lawsuits to recover reasonable fees. Whether fee recovery is available depends on the type of claim, the underlying contract terms, and applicable statutes.

Business Formation

Roquemore Skierski helps Dallas entrepreneurs and established owners form, structure, and scale their businesses with precision, clarity, and long-term protection.

Helping Dallas entrepreneurs form, structure, and scale their businesses with precision and long-term protection.

At Roquemore Skierski, we counsel entrepreneurs, investors, and established companies throughout Dallas and North Texas in all matters of business formation and structuring. Whether you are launching a new venture, reorganizing an existing enterprise, or adding new ownership, our attorneys ensure that your entity is properly formed, compliant with Texas law, and positioned for growth.

Choosing the right business formation lawyer can make the difference between a smooth launch and costly restructuring later. Our firm combines legal precision with practical business insight, guiding clients through every document, filing, and ownership decision from the ground up.

Every formation starts with a conversation about your business model, ownership, and tax priorities. We explain how Texas treats LLCs, corporations, partnerships, and professional entities, then recommend the structure that best fits your goals. We outline the tradeoffs in plain English so your choice is deliberate, not default.

Once the path is set, we prepare and file the core paperwork and organize your internal records. This includes the Certificate of Formation with the Texas Secretary of State, a Company Agreement or Bylaws with Initial Resolutions, organizational minutes and ownership ledgers, any Assumed Name Certificate if you will use a trade name, your IRS Employer Identification Number, and an S Corporation election if it aligns with your tax strategy.

After state approval, we complete post-formation tasks and establish a compliance routine. You will receive guidance on opening accounts and setting internal controls, registering for taxes and insurance, and following a first-year calendar that tracks annual franchise filings and Beneficial Ownership Information disclosures where required.

Selecting an entity is a strategic decision that affects control, taxation, and future financing. We help clients evaluate ownership structure and voting rights among founders, liability protection and separation of personal and business assets, employment and compensation strategy for owners and officers, tax treatment under federal and Texas law, and investor readiness and long-term exit planning.

Our role is to make sure every clause, agreement, and signature reflects a deliberate business choice — not a default form.

After formation, your company must maintain compliance to remain in good standing. Roquemore Skierski provides continuing support as outside general counsel, including annual reporting and registered agent maintenance, updates to operating agreements or bylaws as ownership changes, contract drafting and review for daily operations, employment, vendor, and lease agreements, and guidance on buy-sell terms, capital raises, and restructuring.

Having a consistent business formation lawyer ensures your company evolves legally as it grows commercially.

Our Dallas business formation lawyers guide owners through every stage

01
LLC Registration & Business Formation

Forming an LLC is a critical first step in protecting your personal assets and establishing a strong legal foundation for your business. Choosing the right entity type and structure can impact everything from taxes to liability and future growth.

02
Joint Ventures

Creating a joint venture through an LLC can be a smart way for businesses to collaborate while limiting liability and clearly defining each party's role. A well-drafted operating agreement is essential to avoid misunderstandings and protect both sides.

03
Partnership Formation

LLCs can be an ideal structure for partnerships, combining the flexibility of a partnership with the liability protection of a corporation. By clearly outlining roles, responsibilities, and profit sharing in an operating agreement, partners can avoid future disputes.

04
Operating Agreements

An operating agreement is the cornerstone of any LLC — it defines ownership, management authority, profit distribution, voting rights, and exit procedures. Without one, your business may be subject to default state laws that don't reflect your intent.

05
Governing Rules & Internal Procedures

Clear internal procedures are critical to managing your LLC's daily operations and decision-making processes. These include rules on member voting, meeting requirements, dispute resolution, and handling deadlocks.

06
Membership Changes

As businesses grow, it's common to add or remove members, transfer ownership interests, or restructure internal roles. Without proper documentation, these changes can lead to confusion, disputes, or legal exposure.

07
Annual Compliance

Keeping your LLC in good standing requires more than just formation — it involves ongoing compliance with state filings, recordkeeping, and internal governance. Overlooking these obligations can lead to penalties or loss of liability protection.

08
Exit Planning

Planning for a member's exit — whether through retirement, sale, or unexpected events — is key to protecting the business and remaining owners. A well-crafted buy-sell agreement outlines how ownership changes will be handled.

09
Business Dissolution

Closing an LLC requires more than just stopping operations — it involves legal steps to properly wind down, settle debts, and distribute remaining assets. Mishandling the process can expose members to ongoing liability or tax issues.

Frequently Asked Questions

A Texas LLC or corporation can typically be formed within a few business days once all documents are prepared. The Secretary of State processes standard filings in about two to three business days, with expedited options available. However, total timeline depends on finalizing your operating agreement, obtaining an EIN, and completing any additional registrations.

At minimum you will need a Certificate of Formation filed with the Texas Secretary of State, a Company Agreement or Bylaws governing internal operations, and an IRS Employer Identification Number. Depending on your entity type you may also need organizational resolutions, an Assumed Name Certificate, S Corporation election paperwork, and specific industry licenses or permits.

Texas does not legally require an attorney to file formation documents. However, choosing the wrong entity structure, using a generic operating agreement, or overlooking compliance obligations can create serious liability, tax, and governance problems down the road. An experienced business formation lawyer ensures your entity is set up correctly from the start and tailored to your specific ownership and growth plans.

The right entity depends on your ownership structure, liability exposure, tax goals, and plans for raising capital or adding partners. LLCs offer flexibility and pass-through taxation, corporations provide structure for investor-backed growth, and partnerships suit certain joint ventures. Our attorneys evaluate your situation and recommend the entity that best balances protection, tax efficiency, and operational needs.
